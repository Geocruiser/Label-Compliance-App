# Distilled Spirits TTB Label Rules (Draft)

These draft rules are based on:
- `distilled spirits-labeling-checklist.pdf`
- 27 CFR part 5
- 27 CFR part 16

Use these as implementation rules for label review workflows. This is not legal advice and does not replace full regulatory review.

## Scope

- Applies to distilled spirits labels requiring COLA review.
- Mandatory information must comply with all applicable provisions in 27 CFR part 5 and part 16.

## Rule Set

### DS-01: Same Field of Vision Core Items
- The label must place all three items in the same field of vision:
  - Brand name
  - Class/type (or permitted alternative designation)
  - Alcohol content statement (% alc/vol)
- A field of vision is one side of the container (for cylindrical containers: 40% of circumference) viewable simultaneously without turning.
- Citation: 27 CFR 5.63, 5.64, 5.65.

### DS-02: Brand Name
- The product must bear a brand name under which it is sold.
- Brand name must match the COLA application brand-name entry.
- Citation: 27 CFR 5.64.

### DS-03: Class/Type or Equivalent Designation
- The product must use one permitted designation approach:
  - Class/type designation recognized by regulation, or
  - Trade/consumer understood designation where permitted, or
  - Distinctive/fanciful name with a truthful and adequate statement of composition.
- The designation must be separate and apart from other information and not conflict with other label text.
- If formula-required, the statement of composition must match (or be more specific than) approved formula labeling instructions.
- Citation: 27 CFR 5.141, 5.165.

### DS-04: Alcohol Content Statement
- Alcohol content must appear as percentage alcohol by volume.
- Acceptable abbreviations include `Alc.`, `Alc`, `Vol.`, `Vol`, and `%`.
- If proof is shown, it must be adequately distinguished and must not replace required % alc/vol statement.
- Citation: 27 CFR 5.65.

### DS-05: Net Contents
- Net contents must appear on the label or be blown/marked on container.
- Format must use acceptable metric expressions (for example, `500 mL`, `1.5 L`).
- Container volume must comply with approved standards of fill (generally metric).
- Citation: 27 CFR 5.70, 5.203.

### DS-06: Name and Address
- Label must show the responsible entity name (or trade name) and address (city/state) consistent with permit records.
- Name/address must immediately follow an appropriate role phrase (for example, `Bottled By`, `Imported By`) with no intervening text.
- Citation: 27 CFR 5.66, 5.67, 5.68.

### DS-07: Government Health Warning
- Label must include the exact warning statement required by regulation.
- Formatting and wording checks:
  - `GOVERNMENT WARNING` in capitals and bold
  - Correct punctuation/capitalization (including Surgeon General)
  - Presented as one statement, separate and apart from other information
- Citation: 27 CFR part 16.

### DS-08: Country of Origin (Imported Product Only)
- Imported distilled spirits must include country-of-origin marking compliant with CBP requirements.
- Citation: 19 CFR 134.11; 27 CFR 5.69.

### DS-09: Sulfite Declaration
- If total sulfur dioxide is 10 ppm or greater, label must declare sulfites (for example, `Contains Sulfites`).
- Citation: 27 CFR 5.63(c)(7).

### DS-10: Coloring Material Disclosure
- If applicable coloring materials are used, label must disclose as required (specific coloring material, `artificially colored`, or `certified color added`, as appropriate).
- Citation: 27 CFR 5.63(c)(6).

### DS-11: FD&C Yellow #5 Disclosure
- If FD&C Yellow #5 is used, label must specifically disclose it.
- Citation: 27 CFR 5.63(c)(5).

### DS-12: Cochineal Extract / Carmine Disclosure
- If cochineal extract or carmine is used, label must specifically disclose it.
- Citation: 27 CFR 5.63(c)(6).

### DS-13: Treatment with Wood Disclosure
- For covered whisky/brandy products treated with wood outside normal oak-container contact, label must disclose treatment (for example, `Colored and flavored with wood chips`).
- Citation: 27 CFR 5.73.

### DS-14: Neutral Spirits Commodity Statement (When Applicable)
- If neutral spirits are used in covered blends/rectified products (other than listed exceptions), label must disclose:
  - Percentage of neutral spirits
  - Commodity source from which neutral spirits were distilled
- Citation: 27 CFR 5.71.

### DS-15: Commodity Statement for Neutral Spirits or Certain Gin
- Neutral spirits and gin produced by continuous distillation must state commodity source.
- Citation: 27 CFR 5.71.

### DS-16: State of Distillation (Certain U.S. Whiskies)
- If required by regulation, label must show state of distillation when it differs from state shown in address.
- Citation: 27 CFR 5.66(f).

### DS-17: Age Statement (When Triggered)
- Age statement is mandatory for covered cases, including:
  - Whisky aged less than 4 years
  - Grape lees/pomace/marc brandy aged less than 2 years
  - Distilled spirits with specified age/date representations
- Age format must be one of the approved forms (for example, `__ years old`, `Aged at least __ years`, etc.).
- Citation: 27 CFR 5.74.
